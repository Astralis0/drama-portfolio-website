Synopsis

Etherville by Eamonn Colfer is a short drama that we have studied. It details the story of the main character Elliot Dunne, a car accident victim who's quite severely disabled, barely able to do much with his legs and being confined to a wheelchair. He lives in an assisted living community for disabled people. A nurse and friend of his, Gail !Surname visits him to check up and make sure he's following his recovery plan.

The titular place, Etherville, is a digital place in the virtual world, it's an open source project meaning that anyone can add to it and design things for it, allowing it to flourish and for individuals to escape from the harsh reality that many live in. One of these people that use it to escape from their own harsh reality is Elliot, allowing him to escape his disabilities and live freely in a world where he can walk around in once more. Our Work

We did a number of things based on the play including:

  • A Tableux Vivant[1],
  • A 1 Minute Performance

We were put into groups of three on the 26th of September, my group consisted of:

  1. Myself
  2. Alex O'Brien
  3. Oliver Fagan
    Alex had really wanted to be the director for the group but because there was no two male characters in a scene she couldn't, so Oliver volunteered himself, I had no particular interest in being the director anyways so that's what we went with. Alex and I were both actors for our work and Oliver was the director. On the 2nd of October we performed our pieces, starting with the Tableaux and then the 1 Minute Performance.

Tableaux Vivant

The Tableux Vivant[1-1] that we chose to do was one of the still shots from the opening scenes, specifically the one where Gail is looking for Elliot's emergency alert button and Elliot is just looking defeated

1 Minute Performance

The scene that we elected to do for our one minute performance was the ending scene of the drama, where Elliot is walking using crutches and has gained a happier stance in stark contrast to the beginning of the play.
For the performance we used a number of props to help bring the story to life, which were a set of crutches, a music box and some tables and a chair to build the set.
When deciding the section of the play we wanted to do, we all flicked through the scripts we were given and individually chose a scene, we then took the three scenes we'd chosen and then decided which of them to do. Me and Oliver had chosen the same scene, so we ended up going with that one.

Reflection

I think it was pretty easy to work in a group, at the beginning some people were a little reserved but after the first class and we'd all gotten into it, we worked well together and had fun. It was also nice to work in a smaller group rather than a massive one because it meant that individual opinions were heard and not ignored. My role as Elliot, was comprised of a lot of use of non-verbal communication as Elliot doesn't have many lines in the play apart from his monologue with Sophie. Personally I enjoyed my role because a lot of the expression and acting had to be non-verbal. For the next creative task, I think I could work a bit on rehearsing some actions and things to do while idle, such as when Gail is talking, I also could do a bit with my way of talking as I mostly stuck to my usual tone rather than something different to really embrace his character. I think that we had a pretty good performance overall and I enjoyed working with my team on this.
