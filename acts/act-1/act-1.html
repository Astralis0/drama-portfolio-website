Conception and Development

As a prompt to derive inspiration from for our performance, we were given the scene "What About My Life" from the drama Fences. In said scene we see two characters a husband and wife, the husband complains about being stuck in the same spot for the past few years, the wife gives out to him for this saying that she's been stuck right there with him, and how she's sacrificed so much of her hopes, wants and dreams to be with him. The scene is extremely emotionally charged with both verbal and physical conflict. This emotionality was our inspiration for our drama, and so, we set out to create our own drama piece with heavy emotions and some conflict.
After bouncing ideas around and coming up with different bits, we started with a murder scene for that emotional charge and further developed that into a noir murder mystery. (see summary for more). This throwing of ideas continued for about a class period, it mainly consisted of someone saying "What if we do x" or "Hey wouldn't it be cool if we did y", followed by someone else going sure why not. Eventually we got to the point where we had a whole host of characters, twists and scenes, something that resembled a story. I then wrote a draft of a few scenes and we went through the scenes, changing what didn't flow right, with a bit of improvisation to make dialogue that we all liked.
This is also when we came up with a bunch of different accents and some rough ideas for costumes. We didn't have a specific costume designer for the characters, instead giving everyone the opportunity to create there own costumes that they thought would best suit their character, which I think worked well.
About halfway through the process of developing our piece, every group was advised to stick to one set because the assembling and disassembling of sets would eat into our already limited time. And as such we cut down from 5 or so sets to 3 sets, which was still more than every other group but did require us to rework and remove some scenes. I think this was a good choice because those scenes that were removed weren't essential and were realistically more effort than they were worth.

Rehearsals and Stage Design

Our rehearsals started as just simple reading and learning of lines whilst sitting around a table until we had sets designed, during this time we did checks to see if it was too long or too short, allotting time for actions and stage assembly. Once the stages were designed by Alex Gahan who was our Stage Designer, we then used to build our sets and rehearsed the scenes with full acting and stage set up. Due to the aforementioned cutting of scenes, we had a few stages that were designed which were binned, and while the wasted effort was a pain, it was for the best that we didn't waste the little time we had. Throughout the rehearsals we all did our best to use accents and such, for two purposes:

  1. To practice our skills in varying the usage of our voices, this will stand to benefit us later if asked to change voices.
  2. This also allowed us find the exact right voice for our characters.
    We continued rehearsing the entire drama with full set for a while, once we only had a few more rehearsals till our performance, we moved to using our full costumes and props. For props we had a few fake guns which people had, as well as a knife that I made out of cardstock. We tried to buy as little as we needed to, to make our costumes instead opting for stuff we had at home or if we really needed to, we bought from charity stores.

Summary of the Drama

Our first scene opens with a lone character in an alleyway, Shanice Durkin calls out to a man named Tommy but he's nowhere to be found, instead a mysterious figure enters the alleyway and stabs Shanice in the back. After the mysterious figure flees the scene of the crime, Margaret Durkin enters the alleyway, not realising that her granddaughter has just been murdered in cold blood. She realises what has happened and breaks down in tears.
In our next scene, Bob Brien and Felicia Rider enter the alleyway to the scene of the crime, the examine the body and collect evidence for later inspection.
Our third scene features an interrogation room, with both detectives and Margaret Durkin, she tells them what happens and how Shanice was looking for someone called Tommy, she pleads with them to find who killed her granddaughter.
Continuing with the interrogation room, the elusive Tommy is being questioned, he says how he doesn't know anyone named Shanice but upon being threatened with jail time for lying, he tells the detectives how he was going to meet her but was with another customer at the time. He's questioned as to what he's selling and he asks for his lawyer. His lawyer Fred Squirkgel enters the interrogation room, setting his briefcase on the table, he informs the detectives of Tommy's dog walking business and how he was meeting a customer to walk their dog. Bob opens Tommy's bag and finds a small bag of drugs, he's taken away and the scene ends.
Scene 5, begins in a separate office, Bob sits at a table, illuminated only by a lamp. The table he sits is covered with pieces of evidence, documents, a lone red star that was collected from the crime scene and a bottle of beer. Bob muses about the case and possibilities, before coming to the realisation that the killer is Fred, he bolts up shouting the name and the lights cut to black.
The final scene opens to the detectives re-entering the alleyway and hiding, the two of them bicker with Felicia trying to convince Bob that Fred isn't the killer. Margaret Durkin enters the alleyway with Fred sneaking just behind her. Before Fred can stab Margaret, Bob points his gun at him telling him to freeze. Bob and Fred squabble back and forth which ends with Felicia betraying and shooting Bob. Fred finishes off Margaret and our drama comes to a close.

Reflection and My Role as Scriptwriter and Actor

Overall I think we were very successful in carrying out our first ACT, together as a group and using our given prompt, we created a drama piece that we're proud of (At least I am). We effectively made use of stage and costume design to create our atmosphere, tone and gestures to convey character emotions (For example, Alex used stuttering and scratching to show Tommy's nervousness).
I really enjoyed working on this project as a whole but specifically I enjoyed writing the script for the drama. I found it quite fun to write in a script format as it came surprisingly naturally to me, despite never writing in the style before. In addition to enjoying scriptwriting, I also loved the acting portion, I've always loved to put on little voices or accents, practicing different stances or facial expressions to show different subtexts like the tiny difference between a genuine kind smile or a fake deceptive one. In conclusion I thoroughly enjoyed this ACT and I'm looking forward to the next one as well as any other practical projects that we get to carry out.
